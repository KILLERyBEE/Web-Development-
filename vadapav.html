Vada Pav

Vada Pav is a popular and iconic street food in India, especially in the state of Maharashtra. It is a flavorful and satisfying snack that has become a staple for people of all ages.

Ingredients:

  • Potatoes
  • Gram Flour (Besan)
  • Green Chilies
  • Ginger
  • Garlic
  • Mustard Seeds
  • Turmeric Powder
  • Coriander Leaves
  • Pav (bread)
  • Oil for frying

Preparation:

The vada is made by mashing boiled potatoes and mixing them with spices such as green chilies, ginger, garlic, mustard seeds, turmeric powder, and chopped coriander leaves. This mixture is coated in gram flour (besan) and deep-fried until golden brown.

Serving:

The vada is then sandwiched between pav (soft bread) and served hot. It is often accompanied by various chutneys such as tamarind chutney and green chutney. Some vendors also add a garlic or mint-flavored chutney for extra flavor.

The combination of the flavorful vada with the soft pav and the tangy chutneys makes Vada Pav a beloved and affordable street food that has found its way into the hearts of food enthusiasts across the country.
